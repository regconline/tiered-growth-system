"Should I just use a template?" It's the single most common question we get from South African business owners about to invest in a website. The honest answer is: it depends — but probably not the way you think. Templates aren't always cheap, and custom isn't always expensive. The real question is what your website needs to do for your business over the next three to five years.

Let's break it down properly.

## What "template" actually means in 2026

A template website usually refers to one of three things:

1. **A drag-and-drop builder** like Wix, Squarespace, or Shopify with a pre-designed theme
2. **A WordPress theme** purchased from ThemeForest or similar, then customised
3. **A starter kit or boilerplate** that a developer adapts for you

All three start from a pre-built design and structure. The trade-off is speed-to-launch and lower upfront cost — at the price of flexibility, performance, and long-term scalability.

## What "custom" actually means

A custom website is designed and built specifically for your business from the ground up. The brand, structure, user journey, and code are all created with your goals in mind. There's no theme dictating what's possible — only what's right for your business.

Custom doesn't have to mean built from scratch. Most professional studios use proven frameworks (Astro, Next.js, modern WordPress) but design and engineer the experience uniquely for you.

## The honest cost comparison

Here's what you'll actually pay in South Africa in 2026:

### Template route
- DIY builder (Wix, Squarespace): R200–R600/month + your time
- WordPress theme + freelancer: R8,000–R20,000 once-off
- Premium agency-customised template: R25,000–R50,000

### Custom route
- Boutique starter custom site: R30,000–R60,000
- Mid-market custom site with CMS: R60,000–R150,000
- Enterprise/bespoke build: R150,000+

The price gap is real, but it's narrower than most people think — and the lifetime cost often flips in favour of custom once you account for rebuilds, lost SEO traffic, and missed conversions.

## Where templates win

Let's give templates their due. They are genuinely the right choice when:

- You are pre-revenue and need to validate an idea fast
- You sell a single, simple product and need an MVP store live this weekend
- Your budget is genuinely capped under R10,000 and won't grow soon
- The site is a temporary placeholder for a much bigger project

If any of these describe you, don't overthink it. Spin up a Squarespace site, get to market, and reinvest the wins later.

## Where templates fall apart

The cracks usually appear within 12–18 months:

### 1. Performance ceilings
Most templates ship with bloated CSS, unused JavaScript, and heavy page builders. On a Johannesburg fibre line during peak hours, your site might still take 5–7 seconds to load. Google penalises that, and so do users.

### 2. SEO limitations
Builders give you the basics — title tags, meta descriptions — but they often inject messy code, lack proper schema markup, and force URL structures that hurt rankings. We've seen Cape Town businesses spend years fighting their template just to rank for their own brand name.

### 3. Lookalike syndrome
Your competitors in Pretoria, Sandton, and Durban are using the same five popular themes. When every dentist, accountant, or boutique in your suburb looks identical, you erase your differentiation.

### 4. Conversion compromise
Templates are designed to be flexible for everyone, which means they're optimised for no one. The hero section is generic. The forms are clunky. The mobile experience is "okay" but never excellent. Each compromise costs you leads.

### 5. Hidden long-term costs
That R400/month builder fee adds up to R24,000 over five years — without any equity in the asset. And when you outgrow it, migrating off Wix or Squarespace to a proper platform is painful, expensive, and often loses years of SEO momentum.

## Where custom wins

A properly built custom website pays for itself in three ways:

### 1. Performance you can actually measure
Modern custom builds (using frameworks like Astro or Next.js) routinely score 95+ on Google PageSpeed. That's not vanity — it's measurable conversion lift.

### 2. SEO that compounds
Clean code, proper schema, fast load times, and a sensible URL structure mean your site gets crawled efficiently and ranks faster. A custom site for a Durban professional services firm we worked with went from page 4 to position 2 within nine months on its core keyword.

### 3. Brand differentiation
In a saturated South African market, looking different is a commercial advantage. Custom design lets you express what makes your business specifically worth choosing.

### 4. Scalability without rebuilds
Custom sites are built to grow. Adding a booking system, a portal, a multi-language version, or a new revenue stream doesn't require throwing the site away.

### 5. Ownership
You own the code. You own the hosting. You're not held hostage by a platform's pricing changes or feature decisions made in San Francisco.

## A simple decision framework

Use this matrix:

| Situation | Best choice |
|---|---|
| Validating an idea, no revenue yet | Template |
| Side hustle, evenings only | Template |
| Established business, R100k+ annual revenue | Custom |
| Heavy local SEO competition | Custom |
| Selling high-ticket services | Custom |
| Need bookings, payments, integrations | Custom |
| Simple one-page event or campaign | Template |

## The "premium template" middle ground

There's a growing middle option: agencies (including us, in some cases) starting from a high-quality framework and customising it heavily for the client. This delivers most of the benefits of custom at 60-70% of the cost. For many South African SMEs, this is the sweet spot — you get speed, performance, and a unique design without paying for fully bespoke engineering.

## Real-world example

A boutique law firm in Sandton came to us in 2024 with a R6,500 Wix site they'd had for three years. Bounce rate was 78%. Average time on site was 22 seconds. They were getting 1–2 enquiries per month from the site.

We rebuilt them on a custom Astro stack with proper local SEO, fast load times, and a redesigned consultation flow. Within six months: bounce rate dropped to 41%, time on site tripled, and enquiries went from 2 to 17 per month. The site paid for itself in five months and is still compounding.

## Key takeaways

- Templates are perfect for early-stage validation and tight-budget MVPs
- Custom websites win on performance, SEO, brand differentiation, and lifetime ROI
- The "premium template + heavy customisation" middle ground works well for many SMEs
- Lifetime cost matters more than upfront cost — factor in rebuilds and lost traffic
- If your business is established and competitive, custom almost always pays for itself

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I migrate from a template to a custom site later?**
Yes, but expect to lose 2–6 months of SEO momentum during the migration if it's not handled by an experienced team that understands redirects and structured data.

**Are WordPress sites custom or template?**
Both. WordPress is a CMS, not a design. Most WordPress sites use themes (template) but they can also be fully custom-built using WordPress as the backend.

**How long does a custom website last before needing a rebuild?**
A well-built custom site comfortably lasts 4–6 years with periodic content and design refreshes. Templates typically need rebuilding every 2–3 years.

**Will a custom site really rank better on Google?**
Custom sites have technical SEO advantages (clean code, fast load times, proper schema), but ranking also depends on content and backlinks. Custom gives you the best foundation.
